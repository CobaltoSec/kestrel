# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are professional security practitioners — pentesters, red teamers, and
researchers operating within legal and ethical boundaries. Contributors to
Kestrel commit to maintaining that standard in all project interactions.

## Our Standards

**Expected behavior:**
- Use welcoming, technically precise language
- Accept constructive criticism and give it in return
- Focus discussions on the framework, not on personalities
- Respect that contributors operate under different legal jurisdictions —
  what is legal in one country may not be in another

**Not acceptable:**
- Harassment, trolling, or personal attacks in any form
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Sharing exploit code, credentials, or attack infrastructure in issues or PRs
- Bragging about unauthorized access or glorifying illegal activity
- Misrepresenting Kestrel's capabilities to facilitate attacks outside
  authorized engagements or CTF lab environments

**On scope:** Kestrel is designed for authorized CTF practice (HackTheBox
labs) and professional engagements with written scope. Use against systems
you do not have explicit written authorization to test is a violation of
this Code of Conduct.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported to:
**conduct@cobalto-sec.tech**

All reports are reviewed within 7 days. Consequences range from a private
warning to a permanent ban depending on severity.
